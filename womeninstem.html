The Gender Gap in Tech

What is gender gap?

The "gender gap" refers to the low amount of women and non-binary folks in an industry, group, or field.

In the 1980's, around 30-40% of computer scientists were women. As of now, only a little over 20% of Computer Science earners are women. Although the total number of women in computer science continues to grow, the number of men is only growing larger..

How do we fix this?

Many different initiatives have taken place over the past couple of years that push women into computer science. Some examples are:

The National Center for Women in Information Technology (NCWIT)

NCWIT offers a variety of awards and opportunities to high schoolers, college students, and educators who identify as women or non-binary and show a devoted interest in Computer Science and Information Technology.

Kode with Klossy

Kode with Klossy is a club founded by Karlie Kloss that provides two-week summer camps for 13-18 year olds interested in the coding world. The clubs are beginner-friendly and a great opportunity to make new friends.

Girls Who Code

Girls Who Code is an organization that provides a large variety of tools for students and educators in the programming field; from summer immersion programs, to nation-wide chapters, to college programs, Girls Who Code is working towards making Computer Science a diverse field.

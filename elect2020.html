Kamala Harris

Let's talk about her background, beliefs, and actions.

Kamala Harris is a Democrat and is Joe Biden's chosen Vice President. By being elected, she has become the first of many things. Harris is the first female vice president, asian-american vice president, and black vice president. She served as attorney general of California from 2011-2017, and US senator from California from 2017-2021. By becoming a senator, Harris also became the second African American woman in the senate and the first Asian American woman in the senate. She also ran for president during the 2020 election, but eventually dropped out in December of 2019.

Harris is 56 years old. She was born in Oakland, CA.

How do these policies affect us?

Woman's right to choose

Abortion is the act of terminating a pregnancy if the woman is not ready to or fit to care for a child. Not supporting a woman's right to choose takes away the option of abortion.

Wealth taxes

Supporting wealth taxes supports taxing the wealthy higher than the lower class, but it is not based on just annual income. It is based on the net worth of a person. Most of the time, the only people who are taxed significantly higher are those with a net worth of over a couple million.

Cash bail reform

The cash bail system has been known to ciminalize poverty. People with higher incomes are able to pay the amount given for their bail if they are put in prison.

Increasing capital gains tax rate

Mostly recognized in the stock market, capital gain is the increase of an asset over time. Capital gains go to the highest income households, so a tax on capital gains would make it so less money goes towards those high income households.

Universal background checks

Universal background checks are investigations of someone's background if they want to purchase a firearm.

Healthcare for all

Although both candidates disagree on giving free healthcare for all, Joe Biden is interested in finding another way to provide healthcare for those who can't afford it. Health care for all will help lower income familys stay healthy, but will raise taxes for all Americans.

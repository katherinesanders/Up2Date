I'm not kidding.

Conversion therapy is still legal in 29 states.

What is conversion therapy?

Conversion therapy is any practice that forces a member of the LGBQ+ to converting their sexual identify. These practices cause emotional and even physical harm. Conversion therapy shames (most of the time) CHILDREN into believing they are not normal and/or committing a terrible act. This reinforces self-hatred at a young age, which could lead to mild to severe mental health issues.

Where is it still legal?

Alaska, Montana, Idaho, Wyoming, Arizona, North Dakota, South Dakota, Nebraska, Kansas, Oklahoma, Texas, Minnesota, Iowa, Missouri, Arkansas, Louisiana, Wisonson, Michigan, Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee, Mississippi, Alabama, Pennsylvania, West Virginia, Georgia, South Carolina, and Florida. North Carolina only has a partial ban on conversion therapy.

Why is it still legal?

The main source of homophobia comes from religion. The first amendment protects our right to the freedom of religion, and most of the conversion camps that are still up and running hide behind the religious mask.

What can we do?

Educate yourself. Respect those part of the LGBTQ community. Check if your state has banned conversion therapy, and fight for your state to ban it if it hasn't. Keep the conversation going.
